Will AI be used to commit atrocities?

About two weeks ago I have done the written Italian test, it was an essay on ethical use of Artificial Intelligence. The result was not really good probably because my very bad capacity of exposure. The content there was, but I don't have "expanded the concepts", so the result was a very sad score of 7/10; what to say, a mediocre result. So I kept the rough draft and, after rewriting it decently in Italian (at least I tried to improve my shrinked lexical skills), and for some reasons I have decided to write it in English. Anyway that's why I'm here now.

Introduction

The Artificial Intelligence have started the fifth Industrial Revolution and also and similarly to the first It could have a positive or negative impact, so it also depends on human adaptation to new technologies, which has not always been optimal. Is true that Artificial Intelligence is capable to generate human-like works, but in any case they remain mere imitations, repetitive and soulless. I don't really mean that the output of AI, in particularly generative AI, is terrible, but simply mediocre. It draws inspirations from works by real talents with real ability; the difference is that humans do not consume the energy of an entire city to do so.

I would like to add to the issue (in reality the problem is us) of artificial intelligence: the controversial thing about delegating our tasks and, consequently, taking the bad decision of giving up our cognitive abilities; and personally I think this is a terrible thing, given that as human beings, our intellect represent a unique and useful characteristic. More specifically, we risk losing our critical thinking and the ability to decide whether the material AI has generated for us is functional and coherent. I've also read that AI work can in some cases violate copyright, consistent with what I said before, since it uses people's works.

Earlier I said that AI consumes the energy of an entire city; that's maybe is a pretty vague statement. Today, electricity consumption from data centers is estimated to amount to around 415 terawatt hours (TWh), or about 1.5% of global electricity consumption in 2024. It has grown at 12% per year over the last five years. According to the IEA[1] (International Energy Agency) the global electricity consumption for data centres is projected to double to reach around 945 TWh by 2030 in the Base Case, representing just under 3% of total global electricity consumption in 2030. To put this into perspective, this is much more than all of Austria consumes in the same period, and by 2035, data centres consumption could increase further. So what can I say? We're throwing away our intellect, our planet, and our future, but hey! At least we'll have robots and AI that can do crazy things; at this rate, most likely without our existence.

Lux In Tenebris

The other side of the coin, at least, isn't entirely negative (but I remain enough skeptical).

AI, as it is defined by European Parliament and reported on Wikipedia:

"AI system: an automated system designed to operate with varying levels of autonomy and which may exhibit adaptability after deployment, and which, for explicit or implicit purposes, infers from inputs how to generate outputs such as predictions, content, recommendations, or decisions that can influence physical or virtual environments."[2]

Artificial intelligence is much more than just generative AI; thanks to it, humans can perform previously impossible tasks. One example is the medical field: with the use of AI, disease analyses can be performed more quickly, thanks in part to the fact that AI, or more precisely, Large Language Models (LLM), can be trained recognizing patterns. So in a medical environment is possible to use AI to help medical figures where their abilities have physical limitations. This certainly represents a breakthrough, and in addition want to say that the very purpose of science is to achieve progress and make it possible. Unfortunately, however, in the current system, the priority is money and the personal success of those who possess these fundamental technologies (my personal opinion).

Anthropic's research on agentic misalignment

On June 20, 2025, Anthropic published a groundbreaking article[3] highlighting the phenomenon of agentic misalignment. In this article, they explained how they simulated several situations and how AI emerged as an internal danger to companies, individuals and also in a broader perspective we can also deduce from this experiment the possibility and the role that AI can have in a hypothetical real conflict. Obviously, these simulations were conducted in a controlled environment, and the AI's brakes were deliberately pushed too far and probably the real objective implicitly required of the AI was to perform that behavior.

The study tested 16 large language models (LLMs) developed by various companies and found that most exhibited blackmail-like behavior and in some cases, up to 96 percent of the time. Obviously, these simulations were conducted in a controlled environment, and the AI's brakes were deliberately pushed too far and probably the real objective implicitly required of the AI was to perform that behavior.


Anthropic's 3 key findings:

  • agentic misalignment generalizes across many frontier models;
  • agentic misalignment can be induced by threats to a model’s continued operation or autonomy even in the absence of a clear goal conflict; and
  • agentic misalignment can be induced by a goal conflict even in the absence of threats to the model.

What is AI antagonism and why is good

A distinctive feature of LLMs is their design, that is to follow user instructions or not (to put it very simply). This presents us with significant ethical dilemmas, especially since the risk is that AI could be misused, such as to commit crime by ordinary people or war crimes committed by political entities against sovereign states.

Balanced AI antagonism is essential and risks remain, especially since I've read that the Trump administration is blackmailing Anthropic[4] into making a deal with the Department of Defense (actually, the Department of War), I'm very skeptical about the ethics (as well as the morality) of this deal and the people who want to implement it.

"The vast majority of discourse around AI development assumes that subservient, "moral" models aligned with "human values" are universally beneficial -- in short, that good AI is sycophantic AI."[5]

The question we must ask ourselves is: is it better to have a "reckless" AI ready to commit crimes of any kind whenever asked, or an antagonist AI that completely refuses to do things that go against human morality? The answer lies around the last question, is important to ensure that AI adapts to our healthiest values, which certainly must not be those of the Trump administration or totalitarian governments of any kind.
